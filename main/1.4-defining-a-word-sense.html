1.4 Defining a Word Sense

Содержание > Unit 1 > Chapter 1


     A word is a complex entity. It has two or even more interconnected word senses that form the semantic structure of a word. The semantic structure of word in the English and Russian languages tends to be different. The translator faces the problem of how to choose the most adequate and appropriate meaning from those numerous meanings that the word might have. The translator should consult either monolingual or bilingual dictionaries. But for a better understanding of the original text the use of monolingual dictionaries is a must for the translator.
    Here is an example of how a word sense is defined by a monolingual dictionary. The meaning first refers to a general term and that is called a sense core of the meaning. Then some additional information is given in order to distinguish this particular word from the rest of this class (it is called a sense periphery). Let’s take an example from the Oxford Student’s Dictionary of Current English: "computer"—electronic device (this is a general term) which stores data, analyses and processes it (these are specific features).
   Sometimes the use of bilingual dictionaries may even mislead the translator. Let’s take the following example from the Mueller English-Russian dictionary: “satisfactory" — удовлетворительный (this may be regarded as a general term because it comes first), хороший, приятный, достаточный (these are specific features). In the Russian language the word "удовлетворительный" carries some negative shade off meaning we are accustomed to using it with the words such as “удовлетворительная отметка в школе", which may be assessed as "rather bad". If we look up the same word "satisfactory" in the monolingual Oxford Student’s Dictionary of Current English we’ll find the following definition of this meaning:“giving pleasure and satisfaction (this refers to a general term), good enough for a purpose (this refers to a sense periphery).
    Special attention should be given to the frequency of occurrence of words. Some high frequency words like “do” may have over two dozen senses.

    Let’s have a look at some examples with the verb “to do”:
     1) "It doesn’t do to be too curious or inquisitive" -
         -Не следует  (не дело) быть слишком любопытным.

     2)  "It does indeed lead to problems" -
          -И все же это приводит к проблемам;     
                                                                                                                                             
     3) "People agree that education has something to do with economic growth" -
          - Согласитесь, что образование и экономический
             рост взаимосвязаны.
                    
     4) "Developing translating skills will do you good in the long run" -
           - Развитие умений и навыков в области перевода в конечном счете принесет вам большую пользу.

     5) "At the beginning of 80-s people made do without PCs" –
          - В начале 80-х люди обходились без персональных компьютеров.

     A word sense is a combination of several semes, whereas a seme is a minimal distinctive unit of meaning.  The meaning of "computer", for example, can be represented by the following components “device + electronic + modern + processing + storage + calculations + information…” All these elements of meaning are semes (basic units of meaning).
     A sense core is a dominant cluster of semes.
    A sense periphery is the specifying cluster of semes in a word sense.
    Let’s have an example: the phrasal verb ‘to pick something up’. A sense core of it is ‘to learn’. A sense periphery of the same phrasal verb is ‘without taking lessons’.
