2.2 Change of Word Order

Содержание > Unit 2 > Chapter 2


    The grammatical focus of this chapter is on the broad areas of deviations from the unmarked word order: question formation and inversion.
    In some languages, the word order is very flexible, but this is not the case with English. Deviations from the unmarked word order usually express emphasis of some kind.
    Question formation seems to be rather difficult for the learners of English. Here are some of the problems:
    - Students must remember to invert the subject and the verb, and use the correct auxiliary verb.
     - Students should bear it in mind that the subject of the sentence must always follow the auxiliary verb, no matter how long the sentence is.
     - Students should not hesitate to end a question with a preposition.
   Inversion is an instance of putting words in some special order, thus adding emphasis to any part of the sentence. Adding emphasis is a feature of both the spoken and the written language.
                  
                                             Adding Emphasis

    1)An unmarked word order is where all parts of the sentence are in a ‘non-special’ order.
     Ex.: There was observed a new phenomenon.
          - Наблюдалось какое-то новое явление.

    2) The order can be altered to bring different elements to a stronger position in the sentence.
    Ex.: Never does it lead to considerable changes.
         - Это никогда не приводит к существенным изменениям.

    3) The use of the Passive Voice in the sentence shifts the focus of attention onto the object of the active sentence.
   Ex.: Difficult though it may be the problem will be solved.
        - Как ни трудна эта задача, она будет решена.

     4) Often, by+pronoun is not used in the passive sentence. In case when it is, it remains in a strong position in the sentence.
    Ex.: Hard as it is, the assignment must be done by us.
         - Хотя это задание и трудное, мы должны его выполнить.
                    
    5) Words in the sentences can be reordered and introduced by certain structures to make the important information stand out.

  Ex.: What I like about the new program is that it is very informative.
       - Что мне нравится в этой программе, это то, что она очень информативна.

  Ex.: What annoys me about this method is that it takes a lot of time to carry it out.
       - Что раздражает меня в этом методе, так это то, что на его проведение требуется слишком много времени.

  Ex.: It was he who informed us about the results of their work.
      - Это он (именно он) сообщил нам о результатах их работы.

  Ex.: It is precisely this method that he followed.
       - Как раз этот метод он и использовал.
